Privacy policy

Venux, LLC (hereinafter “Venux”) is committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring you have a positive experience while using Venux services and Venux products.

Venux may change this policy from time to time by updating this page. You should check this page from time to time to ensure that you are fully aware of the changes.

What Venux collects

Venux does NOT collect any of your personal information. Venux merely stores a copy of your encrypted data without being able to access your encrypted data itself.

Venux Remote Services

Venux Remote Services merely propagate your data to the destination specified by you. If encryption services are purchased or requested, data is encrypted but never accessed or shared by Venux.

Access to and Accuracy of Your Personal Information

Venux offers its clients the ultimate privacy. Just as Venux does not have access to the data you store, propagate, or encrypt using Venux services and products, Venux does not have access to your personal information (i.e., login user id and/or password). If you misplace or forget your login user id and/or password, you are out of luck - you are locked out from your Venux account and from the data you stored with Venux. Venux cannot help you. Why? Because, as noted above, Venux does NOT have access to your personal information! Therefore, Venux cannot recover it for you. You will have to start from scratch! Accordingly, Venux encourages you to keep your personal information safe. To update your personal information and communication preferences, you can do so by accessing your profile on the Venux website www.venux.com or by contacting Venux at info@venux.com. Please note that:

  • if you change your login information (login and/or password), synchronization of the data with your new login information may take up to 24 hours;
  • your password has to be at least four characters long (including special characters); and
  • your login user id cannot be the same as your password.

Sharing Your Personal Information

Venux does not sell or share your personal information with anybody simply because Venux does not have access to it.

Venux is committed to protecting your data from an authorized access by utilizing an unparallel proprietary security technologies, procedures, and organizational measures.

Venux will NEVER contact you to request your user id or your password.

Children’s Privacy

  • Venux encourages parents and guardians to go online with their children. Here are a few tips to help make a child’s online experience safer:

Your California Privacy Rights

Residents of the State of California, under California Civil Code § 1798.83, have the right to request from companies conducting business in California a list of all third parties to which the company has disclosed Personal Information during the preceding year for direct marketing purposes. Alternatively, the law provides that if the company has a privacy policy that gives either an Opt-out or Opt-in choice for use of your Personal Information by third parties (such as advertisers) for marketing purposes, the company may instead provide you with information on how to exercise your disclosure choice options.

Although Venux does not disclose any data to any third parties, if you are a California resident and request information about how to exercise your third party disclosure choices, please send a request to info@venux.com.
